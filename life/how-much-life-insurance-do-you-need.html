How Much Life Insurance Do You Actually Need?

Published March 16, 2026 · 8 min read

Most people either buy too much life insurance (and overpay for decades) or not enough (and leave their family exposed). Here's how to calculate the right amount — without a complicated spreadsheet.

The Quick Answer: Rules of Thumb

If you want a fast estimate before diving into details:

  • 10-12x your annual income — the standard recommendation for most families
  • $250,000-$500,000 — minimum for dual-income households with no kids
  • $500,000-$1,000,000 — typical range for single-income families with children
  • $1,000,000+ — if you have a high income, large mortgage, or multiple kids

But these are rough estimates. The right answer depends on your specific situation. Here's how to calculate it properly.

The DIME Method: The Best Way to Calculate

DIME stands for Debt, Income, Mortgage, Education. Add up these four numbers for your coverage amount.

D — Debt

All your non-mortgage debts that would need to be paid off:

  • Car loans
  • Student loans (if private — federal loans are forgiven at death)
  • Credit card balances
  • Personal loans
  • Medical bills

Example total: $35,000

I — Income replacement

How many years of your income does your family need? Multiply your annual income by the number of years:

  • Kids under 5: 15-20 years of income replacement
  • Kids 5-12: 10-15 years
  • Kids 13-18: 5-10 years
  • No kids, spouse works: 3-5 years (or skip entirely)

Example: $75,000/year × 15 years = $1,125,000

M — Mortgage

Your remaining mortgage balance. Paying off the house is the single biggest financial relief for a surviving spouse.

Example: $280,000 remaining

E — Education

Future education costs for your children:

  • Public in-state university: ~$100,000-120,000 per child (4 years)
  • Private university: ~$200,000-250,000 per child
  • Community college → transfer: ~$50,000-60,000 per child

Example: 2 kids × $110,000 = $220,000

DIME Total Example

CategoryAmount
Debt$35,000
Income (15 years)$1,125,000
Mortgage$280,000
Education (2 kids)$220,000
Total coverage needed$1,660,000

Round that up to a standard policy amount: $1.5-2 million in this example.

Subtract What You Already Have

Now subtract existing resources:

  • Current savings and investments
  • Employer-provided life insurance (usually 1-2x salary — check your benefits)
  • Spouse's income (if they'll continue working)
  • Social Security survivor benefits (can be $2,000-4,000/month for families with kids)

In our example, if you have $200K in savings, $150K employer life insurance, and expect $200K in Social Security benefits: subtract $550K. Net coverage needed: ~$1.1 million.

When You Need More Coverage

  • You're the sole or primary earner with young children
  • You have a large mortgage relative to income
  • Your spouse doesn't work or has limited earning potential
  • You have special-needs dependents who'll need lifelong support
  • You own a business with debts or partners

When You Need Less Coverage

  • Dual-income household with no kids
  • Kids are grown and financially independent
  • You have substantial savings (25x annual expenses)
  • Your mortgage is nearly paid off
  • Your spouse could comfortably live on their income + savings

The Bottom Line

Use the DIME method for an accurate number, then buy a round amount (like $500K, $1M, or $1.5M) that covers your needs. Term life is cheap enough that most people should err on the side of buying more rather than less. A 20-year term covers most families through the critical years when kids are young and the mortgage is largest.
