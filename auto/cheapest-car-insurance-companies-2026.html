Cheapest Car Insurance Companies in 2026

Published March 16, 2026 · 8 min read

The difference between the cheapest and most expensive car insurance for the same driver can be $2,000+ per year. That's not a typo. If you haven't shopped your rate in the last 12 months, you're almost certainly overpaying.

We compared average rates from the top insurers to find who's actually cheapest — and more importantly, who's cheapest for your situation.

Who's Actually Cheapest?

Best overall: State Farm

State Farm consistently offers the lowest rates for most driver profiles. They're the largest auto insurer in the US for a reason — their pricing is aggressive, especially for clean-record drivers. They also have local agents in most areas if you prefer face-to-face service.

Best for military: USAA

If you qualify (active military, veteran, or military family), USAA is almost always the cheapest option. Rates are 15-25% below the competition. They also have the highest customer satisfaction ratings in the industry.

Best for digital-first: GEICO

GEICO's online quote process is fast and their rates are competitive. They tend to be cheapest for drivers who want to manage everything through an app and don't need a local agent.

Best for regional: Erie

If you're in Erie's coverage area, they're often the cheapest option — and they have excellent customer service. Their rate lock feature means your premium won't increase after a single at-fault accident.

Who's Cheapest for Your Situation?

Young drivers (under 25)

Young drivers pay the most, period. The cheapest options:

  • GEICO — often 20-30% cheaper than competitors for young drivers
  • State Farm — good student discount can save 25%
  • Progressive — their Snapshot program rewards safe driving from day one

Average rate for a 20-year-old: $3,500-$5,500/year. It drops significantly at age 25.

Drivers with a DUI

A DUI typically doubles your premium. The cheapest companies after a DUI:

  • Progressive — specializes in high-risk drivers, will insure after DUI
  • GEICO — competitive rates even with violations
  • State Farm — may not drop you after a first DUI

Expect $3,000-$6,000/year after a DUI. Rates start improving after 3 years.

Drivers with bad credit

In most states, credit score significantly impacts your rate. Cheapest options:

  • GEICO — weighs credit less heavily than some competitors
  • Progressive — competitive for lower credit scores
  • Nationwide — tends to be more forgiving on credit

7 Ways to Get the Absolute Lowest Rate

  1. Quote at least 5 companies. Prices vary wildly. A 30-minute comparison can save you $500+/year.
  2. Bundle home/renters + auto. Saves 10-25% at most companies. Even renters insurance ($15/month) can unlock this discount.
  3. Raise your deductible to $1,000. Going from $250 to $1,000 deductible saves 15-30% on your premium. Only if you have $1,000 in savings.
  4. Ask about every discount. Most people qualify for 5+ discounts they're not getting. Call and ask.
  5. Pay in full. 6-month or annual payment saves 5-10% vs monthly.
  6. Improve your credit. Going from poor to good credit can cut your rate by 40-50%.
  7. Shop every renewal. Loyalty rarely pays in insurance. Companies raise rates gradually. Switch every 2-3 years.
💡 Pro tip: Get quotes from at least 5 companies every time you shop. Use a comparison tool or get direct quotes from State Farm, GEICO, Progressive, and one local/regional insurer. Takes 30 minutes and almost always saves $300+/year.

The Bottom Line

There's no single "cheapest" company for everyone. But for most drivers with a clean record, State Farm or GEICO will be the lowest. If you're military, USAA wins every time. If you're high-risk, Progressive is your best bet.

The real secret? Shop around. The driver who gets 5 quotes saves $500+/year compared to the driver who renews with the same company for 5 years straight.
